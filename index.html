Margaret Ekpo

Women's right activist

      Chief Margaret Ekpo was a Nigerian women's 
      rights activist and social mobilizer.She was 
      a pioneering female politician in the country's
      First Republic and a leading member of a class of
      traditional Nigerian women activists, many of 
      whom rallied women beyond notions of ethnic 
      solidarity.

Things to note about her:

  • Ekpo was born in Creek Town, Calabar, in July, 1914
  • At age 24, she married John Udo Ekpo, a medical practitioner. She and her husband were vocal about the mistreatment of Nigerian medical staff by the colonial government.
  • In the 1950s, she and Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti mobilized women to be involved in Nigeria’s independence journey
  • During the November 1949 Enugu Colliery strike, Margaret Ekpo helped sustain a protest against the colonial government for the killings of 20 people at an Enugu coal mine. The victims were protesting over unfair dismissals, wages and poor working conditions.
  • In 1954, she established the Aba Township Women’s Association. Through the association, she mobilized women, beyond ethnic and class lines, to fight for their economic and political rights and participate in politics.
  • Margaret Ekpo was also actively involved in Nigeria’s independence struggle. She was a prominent member of the National Council of Nigeria and Cameroons (NCNC) and the only female delegate from the region to represent women’s voices in all the constitutional negotiations.
  • In 1961, Ekpo became the first woman in Aba to be an elected official in the Eastern regional House of Assembly. She served in the House of Assembly until 1967 when the civil war broke out. During the war, she was detained by Biafran authorities for three years.
  • In 2001, the Calabar Airport was renamed in her honour. It is now called the Margaret Ekpo International Airport. It is the only airport in Africa named after a woman.
  • Ekpo died aged 92 in 2006 in Calabar.
